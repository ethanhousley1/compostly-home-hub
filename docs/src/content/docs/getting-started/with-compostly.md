Compostly was built for households that want to compost without managing a pile. We handle the heavy lifting — weekly pickup, processing, and returning finished compost to the community — so you can focus on collecting, not maintaining.

## How the service works

### 1. Collect your scraps

We provide you with a countertop collection bin and durable pickup bucket. Throughout the week, add your organic material to the countertop bin. When it's full, transfer it to the larger pickup bucket and rinse the counter bin for the next round.

Accepted materials include:

- Fruit and vegetable scraps (including citrus and onions)
- Coffee grounds and paper filters
- Tea bags (paper only — remove staples)
- Eggshells
- Bread, grains, and pasta (small amounts)
- Paper napkins and paper towels
- Yard trimmings and leaves

See the complete list in [What to Compost](/docs/guides/what-to-compost/).

### 2. Set out your bucket on pickup day

Place your labeled Compostly bucket at the curb (or your designated pickup spot) by 7 a.m. on your scheduled pickup day. We swap your full bucket for a clean empty one — no washing required on your end.

Pickups happen **every week on the same day**, rain or shine. You can view and manage your schedule in the Compostly app.

### 3. We process your scraps

Your material goes to one of our partnered composting facilities, where it's processed using aerated static pile or in-vessel composting methods. These industrial processes handle materials that home bins typically can't — including cooked foods, small amounts of meat, and compostable serviceware.

### 4. Compost comes back to the community

Finished compost is distributed to:

- **Subscribers** — active Compostly members can claim a bag of finished compost each season through the app
- **Local farms and community gardens** — excess compost is donated to urban agriculture partners in your area
- **Municipal programs** — we work with city composting initiatives to divert organic waste at scale

## What Compostly doesn't replace

Compostly is a pickup service, not a substitute for understanding composting. Many of our members also maintain a small home bin or worm bin — Compostly handles the overflow and items that don't work well in a home setup.

If you're curious about managing your own pile alongside the service, start with [Getting Started at Home](/docs/guides/getting-started-at-home/).

## What's accepted vs. what isn't

### Always accepted

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Fruit & vegetable scraps | All types, including citrus |
| Coffee grounds & filters | Paper filters only |
| Eggshells | Rinse briefly if possible |
| Bread & grains | Keep quantities moderate |
| Yard waste | Leaves, grass, small trimmings |
| Paper products | Napkins, paper towels, cardboard (no wax coating) |

### Not accepted in your Compostly bucket

| Item | Why |
|---|---|
| Plastic bags or liners | Contaminates the batch |
| Compostable plastics (PLA) | Requires industrial certification — contact us |
| Pet waste | Pathogens require specialized handling |
| Liquids | Causes spills and contamination during transport |
| Large bones | Processing equipment limitation |

## Tips for a smooth pickup

- **No bags needed** — don't line your bucket with plastic. If you want to reduce mess, use newspaper sheets or a certified compostable liner.
- **Drain excess liquid** — pour off any standing liquid before setting out your bucket.
- **Freeze smelly scraps** — if pickup day is still a few days away, keep fish scraps or meat trimmings in the freezer and add them on pickup morning.
- **Keep the lid on** — a closed bucket deters pests and keeps your pickup area tidy.
